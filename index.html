Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Students who have passed 10th or 12th standard from a recognised board are eligible to apply for the GDA Nursing course at Sigma Nursing Institute.
GDA Nursing is a 6-month program, while GDA Nursing & Maternity Care runs for 1 year, including both theory and hospital practical training.
The standard fee is ₹21,000. Under our fee waiver program, eligible students pay only ₹3,000 as registration, with the remaining ₹18,000 waived.
Students holding a Yellow or Kesari ration card, belonging to farmer families, or from SC, ST, or OBC categories are eligible for the fee waiver.
Yes. Sigma Nursing Institute provides 100% placement assistance through our network of tie-up hospitals, nursing homes, and patient care centres in Mumbai, Kolhapur, and Goa.
Yes, on successful completion of your final examination conducted by the Skill India Paramedical Institute, you receive a government approved nursing certificate.
Only students enrolled in the GDA Nursing & Maternity Care (1 year) course receive a monthly stipend of up to ₹3,500, starting after the first 6 months of training.
After admission, submit your category proof (ration card or caste certificate). Our team verifies eligibility and applies the waiver directly, so you only pay the ₹3,000 registration amount.
You can apply directly through our online admission form on this website, or contact us on WhatsApp or phone, and our counsellors will guide you through the process.
You will need your 10th/12th mark sheet, Aadhar card, passport-size photos, and category certificate (if applying for the fee waiver).
Theory classes are conducted online through our learning app, taken by experienced faculty, alongside hard-copy study material delivered to you.
Practical training takes place inside our tie-up hospitals, with 8 hours of daily hands-on experience under supervision of experienced nursing staff.
Graduates can work as General Duty Assistants in hospitals, nursing homes, and patient care centres, with maternity care specialists having additional opportunities in maternity wards.
Yes, admission is open to both male and female students who meet the eligibility criteria.
Generally, candidates between 17 and 35 years of age are eligible. Contact our counsellors for specific guidance based on your situation.
